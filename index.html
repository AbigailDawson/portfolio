About Me

Lifelong learning

I grew up in Haverford PA, where my world revolved around reading books, writing stories and spending time with horses. I fell in love with learning Latin in middle school, and in college I dedicated myself to the study of Japanese, which intrigued me even more with its grammatical intricacies and complex characters.

Fascinated by East Asian culture and my new ability to communicate with others in their own native language, I taught myself traditional Chinese and spent a year in Taiwan before returning to purse an M.A. in Asian Studies at Georgetown University.

Exploration

Following this period of rigorous academic study, I dedicated myself to what had previously been a hobby — teaching therapeutic horseback riding lessons for people with special needs, and working on the administrative side of the non-profit organizations operating these programs. Meanwhile, I continued to develop my cross-cultural communication skills while tutoring English online to adult second language learners.

Ultimately, seeking more opportunities to grow personally while learning more about the experiences of others, I embarked on a cross-country trip with my partner and our five pets. Throughout my travels, I experienced life and work in different parts of the country, mixed in with epic mountain biking adventures.

Transitioning into tech

After seeing the impact of my work on an individual level, I was craving a way to make an impact on an even larger scale. With an aptitude for technology and an interest in coding, I made the decision to pivot towards the tech industry, excited to build skills in software development and learn how to create applications and work on meaningful projects. Learning to code has meant equipping myself with the real tools I need to bring my ideas into the world through my own creativity, and contribute to the important work of others before me.
